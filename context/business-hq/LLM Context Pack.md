## Positioning

Hudson Ray works with businesses that are early with AI by sitting with the people doing the work, mapping where time actually goes, and finding where AI belongs. From there, the work usually falls into one of two paths: Build or Activate.

Method:
- Sit with the people doing the work.
- Map where time, judgment, handoffs, and bottlenecks actually live.
- Find the practical places where AI belongs.
- Build the first useful version, or activate the team so people can use AI confidently in real work.

Build line:
I turn "there has to be a better way" into a working tool. I learn how your team works, build the first useful version, and manage the rest when deeper technical help is needed.

Activate line:
I help teams move from AI curiosity to practical, confident use.

Signature contrast:
The context changes. The bottlenecks don't.

The technology is rarely the hard part.

Go-to referral line:
Andre helps CEOs and leadership teams figure out where AI actually fits in the business, then turn the right opportunities into useful tools, workflows, and team capability. He's currently working with the VP of Sales & Marketing at a 129-year-old metals manufacturer on AI-generated website content, commodity pricing modernization, and a custom procurement app.

## Target Buyer

Businesses that know AI matters but have not done much substantial with it yet.

Good-fit buyers often have:

- leaders who are curious but time-constrained
- messy workflows, spreadsheets, old systems, and scattered knowledge
- limited internal AI capacity
- practical business problems, not bleeding-edge AI ambitions
- teams that need confidence, safe use, and useful first steps

Not the primary target:

- AI-native companies
- bleeding-edge AI teams
- companies with mature internal AI/product/engineering capabilities

## Offer Model

1. Free Intro Consult
   - 15-30 minute conversation to understand context and determine fit.

2. Build
   - Turns a messy internal process, workaround, or idea into a working tool, first useful version, implementation-ready plan, or developer-ready spec.
   - Can include workflow mapping, data model design, lightweight internal tools, AI-assisted agents/workflows, implementation support, and coordination of deeper technical help.

3. Activate
   - Helps teams move from AI curiosity to practical, confident use.
   - Can include access/setup, training, safe-use guidance, practice circles, champions, operating rhythm, and adoption support.

Optional diagnostic:
AI Starting Point is a $500, 2-hour working session plus a short brief. It helps the client choose whether the first move should be Build, Activate, or both in sequence. The fee can be credited toward future work.
