# Candidate Market Fit Drafts

Last updated: 2026-06-23

## Purpose

Use this as a working draft for Andre's candidate-market fit and client-market fit. The goal is not to describe everything Andre can do. The goal is to create focused statements that help other people understand where to refer him, hire him, partner with him, or buy from him.

## Guidance From CMF Template

The source guidance emphasizes that a strong CMF should be specific enough that other people can help. People can expand from a focused statement into adjacent ideas, but they struggle to narrow a broad statement into a useful referral.

Useful CMF fields:

- Role / offer / level
- Company domain or focus
- Company type
- Company stage or size
- One cultural attribute

For Andre's business, this translates to:

- Service / engagement type
- Buyer / client type
- Company domain or business context
- Company size / stage
- Cultural or operating attribute

- Avoid presenting as a generalist.
- Establish a singular, clear professional identity.
- Positioning as leadership AI training may be attractive.
- AI consultants are common, so the fit needs to be sharper than "AI consultant."

## Final Fit Statement Options

### 1. Client-Market Fit

Use this when describing the kinds of clients Hudson Ray is looking for.

Statement:
I am looking for established small and mid-sized businesses that are early with AI, curious enough to experiment, and practical enough to want a useful first step. The best-fit clients have work stuck in spreadsheets, email, old systems, or people's heads, and need someone to turn "there has to be a better way" into a working tool, or help their teams move from AI curiosity to practical, confident use.

Shorter version:
I work with established small and mid-sized businesses that are early with AI and want practical value: building useful internal tools or activating teams to use AI confidently in real work.

### 2. Candidate-Market Fit: Company Role

Use this when describing the kinds of companies and roles Andre is looking for if exploring full-time, contract, or fractional work.

Statement:
I am looking for an AI Enablement or AI Process Transformation role at a 1,000-20,000 person company in a stable industry that is committed to AI but not yet fully resourced to execute. The best-fit companies are autonomous, collaborative, future-focused, and need someone who can connect AI strategy, business process, people enablement, and practical implementation.

Shorter version:
I am looking for an AI Enablement or AI Process Transformation role at a stable 1,000-20,000 person company that is committed to AI but not yet fully resourced to execute.

### 3. Secondary Client-Market Fit: Leadership AI Training

Use this when the buyer is asking about safe use, leadership education, or team adoption.

Statement:
I am looking for leadership teams at AI-curious companies that want to learn how to use AI safely and practically, then turn that confidence into first workflows, tools, and team habits.

### 4. Secondary Company-Role Fit: People + AI

Use this when the opportunity is inside HR, Talent, L&D, performance, or people systems.

Statement:
I am looking for People + AI transformation work with organizations that want to improve manager workflows, performance processes, career systems, and employee development in practical, measurable ways.

## Candidate CMF Statements

### Option A: SMB AI Starting Partner

Statement:
I help established small and mid-sized businesses that are early with AI identify the right first opportunity, build or scope something useful, and help their teams put it to work.

Best for:
Website, warm outreach, independent consulting.

Why it works:
Clear, broad enough for multiple industries, narrow enough to describe the buyer stage.

Risk:
"Established small and mid-sized businesses" may still be broad unless paired with examples.

### Option B: Leadership AI Training + Activation

Statement:
I help leadership teams at AI-curious companies learn how to use AI safely and practically, then turn that confidence into first workflows, tools, and team habits.

Best for:
Warm leads like Jennifer/CFO; leadership training opportunities; partner referrals.

Why it works:
Speaks directly to the common buyer question: "How do we use AI safely, and what should we use it for?"

Risk:
Could sound like training only unless the "workflows, tools, and team habits" line stays in.

### Option C: AI Process Transformation For Mid-Market Ops

Statement:
I help mid-market operating companies use AI to modernize messy internal processes, especially where important work lives in spreadsheets, email, legacy systems, or people's heads.

Best for:
Belmont-like clients; operations/procurement/sales process work.

Why it works:
Very concrete and close to demonstrated work.

Risk:
Less obviously tied to leadership training or broader team activation.

### Option D: Full-Time / Fractional AI Enablement Role

Statement:
I am looking for an AI Enablement or AI Process Transformation role at an AI-investing company in a stable industry that is committed to AI but not yet resourced to execute.

Best for:
Recruiters, listening-tour conversations, job/fractional-role discussions.

Why it works:
Directly matches the Mnookin short-term Lane 1 language and keeps the role-market fit crisp.

Risk:
Needs a size/culture modifier to be more actionable.

### Option E: People + AI Transformation

Statement:
I help People, Talent, and L&D teams use AI to improve manager workflows, performance processes, career systems, and employee development in practical, measurable ways.

Best for:
Snap/IBM credibility, HR/People leadership roles, AI-in-HR consulting.

Why it works:
Highly differentiated versus generic AI consulting.

Risk:
Could pigeonhole the independent business too tightly into HR/People.

## Feedback Questions For Advisors

3. Does this match how you understand my strengths?
4. Is the company size/type right?
5. Does this sound too narrow, or usefully focused?
6. What exact person or company comes to mind when you hear this?
