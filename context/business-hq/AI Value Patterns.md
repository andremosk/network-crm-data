# AI Value Patterns

Last updated: 2026-06-24

This is a working taxonomy for explaining the most common ways AI can help a business. It is especially useful in intro calls and AI Starting Point sessions, where clients may know AI matters but not understand the different paths available.

## 1. Customer Product Strategy

AI changes what the business offers to customers.

Patterns:
- Build a new AI-powered product for customers.
- Add AI under the hood to an existing product or service.

Examples:
- Customer-facing recommendation tool.
- AI-enabled portal or assistant.
- Existing software feature enhanced with AI.

Fit for Hudson Ray:
Possible future lane, but not the strongest starting point. This can become more technical, product-led, and engineering-heavy.

## 2. Internal Adoption and Capability

AI changes how people and teams work.

Patterns:
- Help individuals use AI in their daily work.
- Redesign how a team gets work done.
- Roll AI out across a larger group with access, governance, training, and measurement.
- Create a company knowledge brain from internal documents, decisions, SOPs, meetings, and expertise.

Examples:
- Website content generation support for Belmont.
- Commodity pricing workflow redesign for Belmont.
- Team training and coaching.
- Searchable internal knowledge base or assistant.
- Inbox Zero agent that helps a leader or team triage email, identify what needs attention, draft replies, extract tasks, and clear/archive messages with human review.

Fit for Hudson Ray:
Core Activate sweet spot. This aligns with Andre's background in consulting, talent, learning, organizational change, AI enablement, and helping teams trust and use new ways of working.

## 3. Internal Software Development

AI changes how the business builds tools for itself.

Patterns:
- Use AI to build regular software faster and cheaper.
- Turn messy business needs into specs, prototypes, apps, automations, or vendor-ready build instructions.
- Automate or improve document-heavy and admin-heavy processes.

Examples:
- Network CRM product.
- Procurement Hub prototype/spec path.
- Inbox Zero agent connected to Gmail or Outlook.
- Invoice, report, form, contract, spreadsheet, or purchase order automation.

Fit for Hudson Ray:
Core Build sweet spot, especially when paired with business analysis and vendor/developer coordination. The value is not that every final product contains AI; it is that AI makes it easier to create custom tools that fit how the business already works.

## 4. Core Process Improvement

AI changes important recurring business processes.

Patterns:
- Improve sales, marketing, customer service, procurement, operations, HR, finance, or admin workflows.
- Reduce manual handoffs, repetitive drafting, lookup work, and coordination friction.
- Improve customer communication and follow-up.

Examples:
- Sales follow-up support.
- Vendor bid comparison support.
- Customer service triage.
- Proposal or quote drafting.
- Procurement process visibility.
- Email triage, reply drafting, follow-up tracking, and inbox cleanup.

Fit for Hudson Ray:
Strong Build sweet spot. This is often where the clearest business value appears for SMBs.

## 5. Data, Insights, and Decisions

AI changes how leaders understand the business and make decisions.

Patterns:
- Turn business data into trends, summaries, alerts, and recommendations.
- Help leaders make better decisions around pricing, margin, customers, vendors, staffing, inventory, and risk.

Examples:
- Commodity pricing insight.
- Margin and price trend analysis.
- Sales opportunity analysis.
- Vendor performance insights.

Fit for Hudson Ray:
Strong adjacent sweet spot. This becomes especially powerful when combined with Build, process improvement, or a company knowledge brain.

## Hudson Ray Sweet Spots

Primary sweet spots:
- Internal Adoption and Capability
- Internal Software Development

Strong adjacent sweet spots:
- Core Process Improvement
- Data, Insights, and Decisions

Less central for now:
- Customer Product Strategy

## Client-Friendly Explanation

AI can help a business in a few different ways. It can change what you sell, how your people work, how you build internal tools, how core processes run, or how leaders make decisions. The best starting point is usually not the flashiest idea; it is the place where AI can make important work faster, clearer, or easier to act on.

Simple offer translation:
- If the pain is a specific process, tool, spreadsheet, or workaround, the likely path is Build.
- If the pain is team confidence, safe use, access, training, or adoption, the likely path is Activate.
