# Warm Outreach Launch Checklist

Last updated: 2026-06-24

## Goal

Start testing Hudson Ray with warm contacts without waiting for every business detail to be perfect.

## Minimum Setup Before Outreach

### 1. Clear One-Liner

Use:

I work with businesses that are early with AI and want practical value, not hype. The work usually falls into one of two paths: build a useful internal tool, or activate a team so people know how to use AI confidently in their actual work.

Build-specific line:
I turn "there has to be a better way" into a working tool. I learn how your team works, build the first useful version, and manage the rest when deeper technical help is needed.

Go-to referral line:
Andre helps CEOs and leadership teams figure out where AI actually fits in the business, then turn the right opportunities into useful tools, workflows, and team capability. He's currently working with the VP of Sales & Marketing at a 129-year-old metals manufacturer on AI-generated website content, commodity pricing modernization, and a custom procurement app.

### 2. Professional Email

Recommended:
Create a professional email using the working brand/domain once a domain is chosen.

Good formats:

- andre@[domain].com
- hello@[domain].com
- andre@hudsonray.com

Near-term fallback:
Using Gmail is acceptable for early warm outreach if the message is personal, but a custom domain will look more professional before proposals/invoices.

### 3. Simple Web Presence

Minimum viable website:

- one-line positioning
- who it is for
- two paths: Build and Activate
- one clear CTA: book a free intro consult
- short background / credibility
- contact form or email

This can be one page. Do not overbuild.

### 4. LinkedIn Cleanup

Update LinkedIn before broader outreach.

Must-have updates:

- headline reflects AI enablement / practical AI help
- About section aligns with the one-liner
- featured section or post explains Hudson Ray and the problem it solves
- current role can show independent consulting / Hudson Ray if ready

### 5. Warm Outreach Message

Keep it personal and low-pressure.

Template:

Hi [Name] - I’m starting to work with businesses that are early with AI and want practical value, not hype. The work usually falls into one of two paths: building useful internal tools or helping teams use AI confidently in their actual work. Given your role at [company], I’d love to compare notes and hear what AI questions are coming up for you. Would you be open to a quick chat?

### 6. Follow-Up Offer

If they are interested:

Free Intro Consult:
15-30 minute conversation to understand what is going on and whether there is a useful next step.

Paid next step:
Build or Activate, depending on the need. If the right first move is not yet clear, AI Starting Point is $500 for a 2-hour diagnostic working session and short brief. Fee can be credited toward future Build or Activate work.

## Do Not Wait For

- perfect company name
- perfect website
- LLC fully complete, unless needed for invoicing/payment
- polished brand identity
- final pricing for every offer
- perfect case study

## Do Before Sending Proposals

- decide what name appears on proposals
- set up professional email if possible
- confirm entity/payment path
- prepare simple proposal template
- prepare W-9/invoice path once LLC/EIN are settled

## Tax Attorney Meeting Questions

### Entity and Timing

- Should I form a single-member LLC now, or can I safely take early consulting payments as a sole proprietor first?
- Should the LLC be formed in New York given that I live in Suffolk County?
- Is there any reason to form outside New York?
- Does my LLC legal name need to match my public brand?
- Can I use a DBA/trade name if the legal LLC name differs from the public brand?

### Tax Treatment

- If I form a single-member LLC, will it be treated as a disregarded entity by default?
- What are the New York State and New York City tax implications for my situation?
- When would S-corp election start to make sense, if ever?
- At what annual net profit would the S-corp savings likely outweigh payroll/bookkeeping/admin costs?

### Sales Tax and Services

- Are AI consulting, workshops, trainings, or implementation services subject to NY sales tax?
- Does taxability change if I sell software, subscriptions, templates, digital products, or agent/tool access?
- Do I need to register for sales tax now, or only if I sell taxable products/services?

### Getting Paid and Moving Money

- What should I give clients: W-9 with SSN, W-9 with EIN, LLC EIN, or something else?
- Can I invoice Belmont before the LLC is formed, or should I wait?
- How should I transfer money from the LLC to myself?
- What percentage of each payment should I set aside for taxes?
- Should I make quarterly estimated tax payments this year?

### Deductions and Recordkeeping

- Which expenses should I track from day one?
- How should I handle home office, phone, internet, laptop, software, AI tools, mileage, travel, meals, and education?
- What bookkeeping setup do you recommend?
- Do I need a separate business bank account immediately?

### Legal / Risk / Compliance

- Do I need professional liability or E&O insurance?
- What contract terms matter for AI consulting: confidentiality, data handling, client review, IP ownership, deliverables, limitation of liability?
- Are there privacy or data-processing issues I should address in client agreements?
- What should I avoid promising in proposals?

### NY-Specific Compliance

- What do I need to do for New York LLC publication?
- Do I need an operating agreement as a single-member LLC?
- Are there annual/biennial filings or LLC fees I should calendar?
- Do current BOI reporting rules require anything from a domestic single-member LLC?
