## Decisions

### 2026-06-24 - Simplify Public Offers To Build And Activate

Decision:
Simplify the public-facing offer model to two paths: Build and Activate.

Build line:
I turn "there has to be a better way" into a working tool. I learn how your team works, build the first useful version, and manage the rest when deeper technical help is needed.

Activate line:
I help teams move from AI curiosity to practical, confident use.

Rationale:
The previous ladder was useful for internal thinking, but the public website and buyer conversations were starting to feel too complicated. Build and Activate are simpler, more client-friendly, and easier for small business leaders to understand. The model also cleanly separates two common buyer needs: a specific operational pain point that needs a tool, and a team adoption problem that needs training, practice, confidence, and guardrails.

Alternatives Considered:
Keeping Free Intro Consult, AI Starting Point, First AI Build, and AI Team Activation as four public offers.

Risks / Unknowns:
Build is broad and will need clear scope boundaries. Activate may still need tangible modules and examples so it does not sound soft. AI Starting Point should remain available as a diagnostic, but not clutter the main website architecture.

Next Actions:
Update website structure around Build and Activate. Keep Free Intro Consult as the primary CTA. Use AI Starting Point selectively when the client needs help choosing the right path.

Agent Memory:
Prefer plain small-business language. Avoid overexplaining platforms and technical details unless the client asks. Position Andre as someone who can build a lot directly and coordinate/project-manage deeper technical help where needed.

### 2026-06-03 - Set AI Starting Point Price

Decision:
Set AI Starting Point at $500, with the fee available to credit toward a future First AI Build.

Rationale:
The price is simple, approachable, and credible for an early paid offer. It is higher than a very casual working session while still low enough for a business owner or leader to say yes without a heavy approval process. Crediting it toward a future build reduces friction while preserving the value of the session.

Alternatives Considered:
$300 to mirror Houston AI Help's working session more closely; $750-$1,000 for a more premium strategy session.

Risks / Unknowns:
The offer may be underpriced if the written brief becomes too time-intensive. The price can be revisited after a few real client conversations or sessions.

Next Actions:
Define the AI Starting Point intake questions, session agenda, and brief template.

Agent Memory:
Keep pricing simple for now. Avoid complex tiers or policies until the offer has been tested.

### 2026-06-03 - Choose Working AI Consulting Brand Name

Decision:
Use AM AI Help as the working brand name for now.

Rationale:
The name is personal, plainspoken, approachable, and directly signals AI help. It fits the current positioning around helping businesses get meaningfully started with AI without sounding too formal or overbuilt.

Alternatives Considered:
KBAM Advisory, AM AI, AM PM AI, Buttercup AI, The AI Guy NY, AM AI Advisory.

Risks / Unknowns:
AM AI Help may signal a slightly smaller or less premium offer than advisory-focused names. The name should be revisited after offer details, pricing, and website copy are clearer.

Next Actions:
Use AM AI Help as the working name while defining offer details and client-facing language.

Agent Memory:
The user prefers approachable, plainspoken naming with room for personal meaning. "Advisory" may be too formal. The brand should serve businesses that know AI matters but have not yet done much substantial with it.

### 2026-06-02 - Define Initial Offer Ladder

Decision:
Use the following initial offer ladder:

1. Free Intro Consult
2. AI Starting Point
3. First AI Build
4. AI Team Activation

Rationale:
This gives prospective clients a simple path from low-pressure conversation to paid scoping, then into hands-on workflow work or broader team activation. It also keeps the offer flexible enough for Andre to build directly, scope developer-ready work, or coordinate with vendors depending on the use case.

Alternatives Considered:
AI Opportunity Scan, AI Workflow Build, AI Action Plan, AI Starting Point Session, Practical AI Workshop, First Use Case Session, AI Workflow Sprint, AI Implementation Sprint.

Risks / Unknowns:
Offer names may need refinement once tested with real prospects. Pricing, deliverables, and boundaries still need to be defined.

Next Actions:
Define the deliverables, pricing hypothesis, and client-facing descriptions for each offer.

Agent Memory:
The business should avoid overclaiming around production-grade implementation. The promise is practical AI progress: discover the right opportunity, shape it clearly, build or coordinate something useful, and activate value with the team.
