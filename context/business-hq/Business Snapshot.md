# Business Snapshot

Last updated: 2026-06-24

## Current Situation

You have started independent consulting work and expect to be paid soon. There is one current client, and the business needs both a practical setup for receiving payment and a path toward additional clients.

## Current Client Work

Current client: Belmont Metals, a non-ferrous metals manufacturer.

The work spans three active projects:

1. Procurement Hub project management
   - Acting as the business-side project manager for an AI-designed Microsoft Power Apps procurement management system.
   - Translating business requirements into delivery oversight, UAT planning, issue tracking, decision tracking, and go/no-go recommendations for milestone payments.
   - Helping Belmont make sure a custom procurement workflow gets built correctly across requisitions, vendor bids, UOM conversion, index-plus-premium pricing, audit trails, and purchase history.

2. Commodity pricing discovery and future-state architecture
   - Documenting a complex daily metals pricing workflow currently dependent on a long-tenured internal expert, Reuters/LSEG Eikon, Excel, Outlook archives, and manual methodology.
   - Turning tribal knowledge into a developer-ready specification covering RIC codes, alloy calculations, specialty metals sources, cell maps, historical price extraction, and cloud automation options.
   - Defining a future-state architecture for cloud-based commodity pricing using LSEG Data Library API, Microsoft Dataverse, Power Automate, SharePoint, and structured review/approval workflows.

3. AI-assisted website product metadata generation
   - Using LLMs to generate structured technical product data for a new Material Selector on Belmont's website.
   - Designing and operating repeatable prompt workflows for alloy categories, including melting points, density, lead-free status, specifications, primary elements, feature tags, solubility notes, and engineer-facing FAQs.
   - Reviewing AI-generated outputs for accuracy, structure, and usability before metallurgist and internal review.

## Current Client Outcomes

Belmont is paying for modernization in the sales and procurement parts of the business, with the aim of improving profit, visibility, decision-making, and operational confidence.

1. Procurement Hub
   - Outcome: clearer line of sight into the procurement process.
   - Business value: leadership can see whether the procurement team is following best practices, such as seeking multiple bids, and the team can more easily look up requisition and bid history.
   - Underlying value: better procurement discipline, better auditability, less process opacity, and more confidence in purchasing decisions.

2. Commodity Pricing
   - Outcome: preservation of critical pricing knowledge and a path toward a more automated pricing system and historical pricing database.
   - Business value: Belmont reduces dependency on one expert and one desktop setup while improving access to current commodity prices.
   - Underlying value: sellers avoid leaving money on the table in customer agreements, and procurement can negotiate with vendors using more timely pricing context.

3. Website Product Data
   - Outcome: messy product information becomes structured technical content that can power the new website and Material Selector.
   - Business value: customers can find the right alloys more easily, and Belmont can create better sales follow-on opportunities.
   - Underlying value: product data becomes a sales asset rather than static catalog content.

Overall client value:

Belmont is paying to modernize its sales and procurement technology stack so it can protect margins, improve procurement discipline, generate better operational insight, and create more sales opportunities.

## Working Offer Model

Build or Activate.

The front door is a free intro consult. If the client is not sure where to begin, AI Starting Point can be used as a paid diagnostic session. The two main paths are:

Build:
Turn "there has to be a better way" into a working tool. Learn how the team works, map where time actually goes, build the first useful version, and manage the rest when deeper technical help is needed.

Build is for work trapped in spreadsheets, email, old systems, or people's heads. It can include workflow mapping, data model design, AI-assisted tool building, lightweight internal apps, developer-ready specs, vendor coordination, implementation support, and handoff documentation.

Activate:
Move teams from AI curiosity to practical, confident use by grounding the work in their real day-to-day tasks. This can include access setup, training, safe-use guidance, live practice, peer learning, coaching, AI champions, and adoption support.
