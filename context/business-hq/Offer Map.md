# Offer Map

Last updated: 2026-06-24

## Positioning Anchor

Hudson Ray works with businesses that are early with AI by sitting with the people doing the work, mapping where time actually goes, and finding where AI belongs.

The context changes. The bottlenecks don't.

The technology is rarely the hard part.

## Simple Offer Model

Build or Activate.

The front door is still a free intro consult. From there, clients can choose the path that matches their need:

- **Build:** turn a messy internal process, workaround, or idea into a working tool.
- **Activate:** help a team understand, trust, and use AI in practical ways.

AI Starting Point can still be used as a paid diagnostic session when the client is not sure which path comes first.

## Front Door: Free Intro Consult

Format:
15-30 minute free conversation.

Operating toolkit:
See Free Intro Consult Toolkit.md for the call flow, qualification checklist, follow-up emails, and transition language.

Purpose:
Get to know each other, understand what prompted the client to explore AI, and decide whether there is a useful next step.

Best for:
- Leaders who are curious about AI but unsure where to start.
- Businesses with a specific pain point, workflow, or team need.
- Potential clients who need a low-pressure first conversation.

Client gets:
- A clearer sense of whether AI may be useful for their situation.
- A recommendation on whether Build, Activate, or an AI Starting Point session makes sense.
- A simple next step.

Notes:
This is not the place to solve the whole problem. It is a fit check and orientation conversation.

## Path 1: Build

Core line:

I turn "there has to be a better way" into a working tool. I learn how your team works, build the first useful version, and manage the rest when deeper technical help is needed.

Plain-English version:

For teams with work stuck in spreadsheets, email, old systems, or people's heads, Build starts by learning how the work really happens. Then it turns the messy process into something easier to run.

Purpose:
Create a practical internal tool, workflow, automation, agent, or developer-ready build plan that helps the business run better.

Best for:
- A business process that is too manual, too scattered, or too dependent on one person.
- A leader who knows the work should be easier but does not know what the tool should be.
- A team that needs someone to translate the process, design the system, build the first useful version, and coordinate specialist help where needed.

What this can include:
- Workflow mapping and process definition.
- Data model or spreadsheet/database design.
- AI-assisted tool or agent build.
- Lightweight internal app or prototype that can become a working system.
- Requirements, build spec, and vendor/developer coordination.
- Implementation support with a client's IT/admin partner.
- Handoff documentation and team onboarding.

Client gets:
- A clarified workflow.
- A practical first version, build plan, or developer-ready specification.
- Clear next steps for implementation.
- Documentation and handoff materials.
- Coordination of deeper technical help when needed.

Operating toolkit:
See First AI Build Toolkit.md for the Build delivery guide: fit criteria, engagement phases, deliverables, pricing hypotheses, scope guardrails, and Build Brief template.

## Path 2: Activate

Core line:

I help teams move from AI curiosity to practical, confident use.

Plain-English version:

For teams that know AI matters but are not using it meaningfully yet, Activate starts with the actual work people do every day. Then it gives them the examples, practice, guardrails, and coaching to start using AI with confidence.

Purpose:
Help a team understand what AI is useful for, use it safely, practice on real work, and build habits that stick.

Best for:
- Leaders asking, "What should we actually use AI for?"
- Teams that need safe-use guidance and practical examples.
- Organizations where adoption, trust, and confidence are the main blockers.
- Larger groups where the goal is not one tool, but capability across more people.

What this can include:
- AI readiness and access setup.
- Practical AI training for leaders and teams.
- Safe-use guidance and simple guardrails.
- Role-specific examples and exercises.
- Peer learning circles or office hours.
- AI champions / power-user support.
- Adoption metrics and operating rhythm.

Client gets:
- Clear guidance on where AI fits into the team's work.
- Live training and hands-on practice.
- Simple rules for safer use.
- Team examples and reusable patterns.
- Coaching as people build their own first use cases.
- Next-step recommendations for deeper build opportunities.

Operating toolkit:
See AI Team Activation Toolkit.md for the Activate delivery guide: modules, deliverables, pricing hypotheses, activation metrics, and scope guardrails.

## Optional Diagnostic: AI Starting Point

Format:
2-hour paid working session.

Price:
$500. If the client moves forward into a Build or Activate engagement, the fee can be credited toward that work.

Purpose:
Help a client who knows AI matters but is not sure where to begin choose the right first path.

Short version:

A two-hour working session to find the best place for your business to start with AI and turn it into a practical next-step plan.

Core promise:

The client leaves knowing where to start, why that starting point makes sense, and what to do next.

Client gets:
- A map of relevant business pain points and AI opportunities.
- 2-4 candidate AI opportunities.
- A recommended first path: Build, Activate, or both in sequence.
- A plain-English outline of what to build, change, automate, document, or teach.
- A short AI Starting Point Brief.

Operating toolkit:
See AI Starting Point Toolkit.md for the intake questions, session agenda, scoring rubric, brief template, follow-up email, and transition language.

## Current Offer Logic

The free consult creates trust and fit.

Build turns a specific operational pain point into a working tool or clear implementation path.

Activate helps teams capture value from AI through training, practice, confidence, guardrails, and adoption support.

AI Starting Point is a useful paid diagnostic when the client needs help choosing the right first move.
